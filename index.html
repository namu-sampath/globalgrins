Global Grins: How happy are we worldwide?

A breakdown of how happy countries are based on data provided by the 2022 World Happiness Report.

By Namu Sampath

July 24, 2023

Source: The New York Times.

Did you know that March 20 is International Day of Happiness?

Collectively across the globe, people are becoming much more curious about human happiness, health and wellbeing. Per data scraped from the World Happiness Report, Finland, Denmark and Iceland are the happiest countries in the world.

The United States is the 16th happiest country, sandwiched between Canada and the United Kingdom. Generally, Americans have a high life expectancy score (0.783), quite a bit of freedom of choice (0.574), are generous people (compared to other countries in the top 10).

The 2022 report compares 146 countries of 195 worldwide. Some countries, like Syria, are not included due to a lack of data being readily available in years' past.

The report builds its analysis by looking at six categories: dystopia, GDP per capita, healthy life expectancy, freedom to make life choices, generosity and perception of corruption. The overall happiness score is then determined by finding the sum of these six categories.

Source: The World Happiness Report.

Eight of the top 10 happiest countries in the world are, perhaps surprisingly to some, are European countries. The ninth happiest country, Israel, is located in Asia, though unlike other Asian countries, has a relatively high score for "life expectancy" (which is scored in decimal points between 0 and 1).

Comparatively, the 10 unhappiest countries in the world are predominantly African countries, with the exception of two: Lebanon and Afghanistan, which, according to the report are classified as being located in the "Middle East."

In the chart above, the countries that are the unhappiest are in the top left corner, and the warmer the color gets, the happier the countries are. The lowest happiness score in 2022 is 2.404 for Afghanistan (which is denoted as the top right square), whereas the darkest orange squares are denoted as Finland, Denmark and Iceland.
